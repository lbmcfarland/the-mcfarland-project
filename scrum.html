Scrum Master. This TCP block of instruction is to prepare you for taking the Scrum.org Professional Scrum Master I (PSM I) certification exam. Scrum is an Agile framework for approaching software development (among other things). This much will become clear in class. Special Terminology

You need to be familiar with these terms as they may be referenced on the exam. Some are not required for use by scrum (e.g., velocity and burn-down chart), burn-down chart was tested on my exam, and velocity appeared as a multiple choice option. Knowing what they mean will help you quickly choose or eliminate them without needing to waste time searching.

Burn-down Chart. A chart which shows the amount of work which is thought to remain in a backlog. Time is shown on the horizontal axis and work remaining on the vertical axis.

Definition of Done. A shared understanding of expectations that the Increment must live up to in order to be releasable into production. Created and managed by the Development Team.

Empiricism. Process control type in which only the past is accepted as certain and in which decisions are based on observation, experience and experimentation. Empiricism has three pillars: transparency, inspection and adaptation.

Increment. A piece of working software that adds to previously created Increments, where the sum of all Increments - as a whole - form a product.

Stakeholder. A person external to the Scrum Team with a specific interest in and knowledge of a product that is required for incremental discovery. Represented by the Product Owner and actively engaged with the Scrum Team at Sprint Review.

Technical Debt. The typically unpredictable overhead of maintaining the product, often caused by less than ideal design decisions, contributing to the total cost of ownership. May exist unintentionally in the Increment or introduced purposefully to realize value earlier.

Velocity. An optional, but often used, indication of the average amount of Product Backlog turned into an Increment of product during a Sprint by a Scrum Team, tracked by the Development Team for use within the Scrum Team.

Test-taking and Study Tips

Exam. The exam has 80 questions, and you have one hour to complete it. Each question must have a response, so you must get through the entire test. In addition, the test is open book and open resources. With this in mind, I recommend the following when taking the test.

  • Go through the entire test and respond with your best guest.
  • Take a sheet of paper into the exam and make a note of the question numbers you want to revisit for further research/thought.
  • Have a digital copy of the scrum guide open and search it using keywords from the question you are looking to answer. In the digital copy, the table of contents items are linked to their sections in the guide. So, for an example, if you needed to look up the definition of scrum, you could click on that item in the table of contents, and it would take you right to that page. This is a huge time-saver.
  • If you are really struggling with a question, copy/paste (or type if this can't be done) the entire question into Google. Pick the search result sourced at a scrum.org page (e.g., blog post, etc.) for the answer.
